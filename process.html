How I Build Products That Matter

A systematic approach to product development that combines strategic thinking, user research, and iterative design to deliver measurable business outcomes.

MY PHILOSOPHY

Product management is about solving the right problems, not building features.

I believe great products emerge from a deep understanding of user needs, business goals, and technical constraints. My approach centers on continuous discovery, rapid experimentation, and data-driven decision making.

Every product decision should ladder up to measurable impact—whether that's revenue growth, user satisfaction, or operational efficiency.

01
Start with Why
Deeply understand the problem before jumping to solutions. Question assumptions and validate hypotheses.
02
Bias Toward Action
Ship early and often. Learn from real users rather than endless planning and speculation.
03
Measure What Matters
Define success metrics upfront. Let data guide decisions, but don't ignore qualitative insights.
04
Design for Scale
Build products that work today but can grow tomorrow. Balance technical debt with speed to market.

My Product Development Process

A flexible framework that adapts to each project's unique constraints while maintaining focus on user value and business impact.

PHASE 01
01

Discovery & Research

Understanding the problem space through user research, market analysis, and stakeholder alignment. This phase ensures we're solving the right problem before investing in solutions.

→ User interviews and contextual inquiry
→ Competitive analysis and market research
→ Stakeholder workshops and alignment sessions
→ Opportunity sizing and business case development
PHASE 02
02

Strategy & Prioritization

Defining the product vision, setting clear objectives, and prioritizing initiatives based on impact and effort. This creates alignment across teams and ensures resources are focused on high-value opportunities.

→ Product vision and strategy documentation
→ OKR definition and success metrics
→ Feature prioritization using RICE or ICE frameworks
→ Roadmap development and communication
PHASE 03
03

Design & Validation

Rapid prototyping and user testing to validate solutions before heavy engineering investment. This iterative approach reduces risk and ensures we're building something users actually want.

→ Wireframing and low-fidelity prototypes
→ Usability testing and feedback sessions
→ High-fidelity design and interaction design
→ Technical feasibility assessment with engineering
PHASE 04
04

Build & Iterate

Working closely with engineering to ship incrementally, gathering feedback, and iterating rapidly. Clear communication and realistic scope management ensure quality delivery.

→ Sprint planning and backlog grooming
→ Daily standups and cross-functional collaboration
→ Progressive rollout and feature flags
→ QA coordination and bug triage
PHASE 05
05

Launch & Measure

Coordinating go-to-market activities, monitoring key metrics, and gathering user feedback. Launch is just the beginning—continuous monitoring reveals opportunities for optimization.

→ Launch planning and stakeholder communication
→ Analytics instrumentation and dashboard setup
→ A/B testing and experimentation
→ Post-launch user interviews and surveys
PHASE 06
06

Learn & Optimize

Analyzing performance against goals, identifying areas for improvement, and feeding learnings back into the product cycle. Great products are never "done"—they evolve with user needs.

→ Performance review against KPIs
→ Retrospectives and team learnings
→ Continuous optimization and iteration
→ Documentation and knowledge sharing

How I Collaborate

Product management is a team sport. Here's how I work with different functions to deliver great products.

👥

With Engineering

I believe in partnership, not gatekeeping. I work closely with engineering to understand technical constraints, explore creative solutions, and make informed trade-offs. Clear requirements, realistic timelines, and open communication build trust and velocity.

🎨

With Design

Great products need great design. I collaborate with designers from discovery through implementation, ensuring user needs drive visual and interaction decisions. I provide context and constraints while giving designers space to explore creative solutions.

📊

With Data/Analytics

Data informs decisions but doesn't make them. I partner with analysts to define metrics, instrument tracking, and interpret results. I ask the right questions and challenge assumptions to ensure we're measuring what actually matters.

💼

With Business Stakeholders

I translate between technical possibilities and business goals. Regular updates, clear roadmaps, and honest communication about trade-offs keep stakeholders aligned and confident. I advocate for users while respecting business constraints.
